01 — Brand strategy
What Pakka Quote stands for

Before any logo or color — the brand needs a clear point of view. Everything visual flows from this.

Positioning statement
For Indian traders and manufacturers who lose deals because they respond too slowly — Pakka Quote is the proposal system that works on WhatsApp, so you send professional quotes in minutes, not days.
Unlike generic CRM tools that require behavior change, Pakka Quote meets the owner where they already are — no app, no dashboard, no training. Just forward the inquiry and approve the quote.
02 — The name
Why "Pakka Quote" works

"Pakka" (पक्का) is one of the most loaded words in Indian business culture. It means confirmed, solid, trustworthy, for real. When a trader says "pakka deal" — it's done. The name earns trust instantly with the target audience and is completely unmemorable to anyone outside it, which is a feature, not a bug.

03 — Logo
Logo system

The logo mark is a checkmark (✓) styled as a stamp — evoking the "pakka" seal of confirmation. Paired with the Syne wordmark in heavy weight. The dot after "Pakka" is a deliberate pause — like the moment of confirmation.

Logo mark construction

The icon is a rounded square (8px radius at 36px) in Pakka Saffron with a white bold checkmark. The check stroke is 3px at 36px, scaled proportionally. Corner radius is always 22% of the icon size.

In lockup, the icon and wordmark are vertically centered. Gap between icon and type is exactly the cap-height of the wordmark. Never stretch or recolor the icon.

04 — Color
Color palette

Rooted in Indian visual culture — saffron as the hero, warm neutrals for trust, deep ink for authority. No cold blues or techy purples. This palette feels familiar to a trader in Surat or a manufacturer in Ludhiana.

Color logic
Saffron is used for one thing: action. Buttons, CTAs, the logo mark. Never as a background for large areas. Trade Ink is used for all serious text. Deal Green only appears when something good happens — a proposal sent, a deal confirmed. Cream is the default surface — warm, not cold white.

07 — WhatsApp copy
Message voice in product

The product lives on WhatsApp. Every message Pakka Quote sends must feel human — not like a bot, not like a notification. Short. Scannable. Actionable. Here's the approved tone for key messages.

📋 New inquiry — +91 98XXX XXXXX Product: TMT Fe500, 12mm Qty: 500 kg Delivery: Surat · Tuesday Urgency: High ⚠️ Rate per kg?
68.50
📄 Draft ready Sharma Steel → Buyer 500 kg TMT Fe500 @ ₹68.50 Total: ₹34,250 + 18% GST Delivery: Surat · Tuesday SEND · EDIT · SKIP
SEND
✅ Sent. Follow-up: Day 2, 5, 10.
Message rules: No full stops at the end of lines inside bubbles. Emoji used sparingly — only 📋 ✅ ⚠️ 📄 in product messages. Numbers always with ₹ and commas (₹34,250). Urgency only flagged with ⚠️ when delivery is within 48 hours.

09 — Usage rules
Brand dos and don'ts
Do
Always write "Pakka Quote" with a capital P and capital Q — never "pakka quote" or "PAKKA QUOTE"
Don't
Never use the logo on a colored background other than white, cream, or Trade Ink dark
Do
Use Saffron only for the logo, buttons, and key accents — never as a large background fill
Don't
Never use "AI-powered" in primary messaging — let the speed and quality speak. Rajesh doesn't care about AI.
Do
Lead with the outcome: "proposal in 2 minutes", "deal won", "no deal lost to slow response"
Don't
Never use the words: "revolutionize", "disrupt", "synergy", "leverage", "ecosystem" — anywhere
Do
Mix English and Hindi naturally in marketing — this is a feature of the brand, not a mistake
Don't
Never call it "software" or "platform" in owner-facing copy. Call it "a system" or just describe what it does.
