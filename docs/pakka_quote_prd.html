01 — Problem

Why this exists

Indian manufacturing and trading SMBs — steel, textiles, chemicals, FMCG — operate in high-velocity B2B environments where speed of response directly determines whether a deal is won or lost. The status quo is broken in five specific ways.

1
Inquiries arrive via WhatsApp and email at all hours. Responses take 1–3 days. By then, the buyer has moved on.
2
The business owner is the bottleneck. Pricing lives in their head, not in any system. Nothing can move without them.
3
Proposals are informal, inconsistent, and unbranded — sent as WhatsApp text or rough Excel sheets. No standard format, frequent errors.
4
There's no follow-up discipline. Deals die in silence because no one remembered to nudge the buyer on day 3.
5
No pipeline visibility. The owner doesn't know how many open inquiries exist, which are warm, or which were never responded to.
02 — Users

Who we're building for

One primary user. One secondary. The product is designed around the primary user's existing behavior — not around asking them to adopt new habits.

Primary — The SMB Owner

Rajesh, 38–52

Runs a manufacturing or trading business with ₹1–10 crore annual revenue. Handles 5–30 inquiries per week. Speaks Hindi/Hinglish. Lives on WhatsApp. Distrusts software that requires behavior change or training.

Key insight: He will use Pakka Quote if and only if it requires fewer taps than what he does today. If it adds friction, he abandons it.

Secondary — The Buyer

The inquiry sender

A procurement manager, trader, or retailer placing inquiries via WhatsApp or email. Doesn't know or interact with Pakka Quote directly. Receives a professional PDF proposal from the SMB owner.

Key insight: Their experience improves because they get a faster, more professional response. They never know Pakka Quote exists.

User stories
SMB Owner
When a new inquiry arrives on my WhatsApp, I want to forward it and receive a ready-to-send proposal within minutes, so that I don't lose the deal to a competitor who responds faster.
SMB Owner
When I receive a parsed inquiry summary, I want to type one number (my price) and say SEND, so that I stay in control without doing administrative work.
SMB Owner
When I haven't heard back from a buyer, I want automatic follow-ups sent on my behalf, so that I recover deals I would otherwise forget.

05 — Conversation design

The three conversations

Pakka Quote has three distinct conversation types, each with a different person at one end. Getting these right is the core design challenge.

Conversation 1 — Onboarding (happens once)

Collects the owner's catalog, payment terms, GST details, and branding. 5–8 questions. Must complete in under 5 minutes.

Onboarding flow
Pakka Quote
Welcome to Pakka Quote. Let's set you up in 5 minutes. What's your business name and GST number?
Rajesh
Sharma Steel Traders GST 27AABCS1234A1Z5
Pakka Quote
Got it. Tell me your top 3–4 products with typical sizes or grades you quote most often.
Rajesh
MS Pipes 1 to 4 inch, TMT Bars Fe500, GI Sheets 0.5mm to 2mm
Pakka Quote
What are your standard payment terms? (e.g. 50% advance, balance before dispatch)
Rajesh
30 days credit for regular customers, advance for new ones
Pakka Quote
You're set up. Forward me any inquiry and I'll handle it from here.

Conversation 2 — Inquiry handling (the core loop)

Triggered every time an inquiry arrives. Owner does two things: give a price, say SEND. This conversation must feel instant and frictionless.

Inquiry → proposal flow
Rajesh (forwarding customer message)
bhai 500 kg TMT Fe500 12mm chahiye, Tuesday tak mil sakta hai kya? Surat delivery
Pakka Quote
New inquiry from +91 98XXX XXXXX Product: TMT Bars Fe500, 12mm Quantity: 500 kg Delivery: Surat, by Tuesday Urgency: High What price per kg do you want to quote? (or type "skip" to ignore)
Rajesh
68.50
Pakka Quote
DRAFT PROPOSAL Sharma Steel Traders → Buyer TMT Fe500 12mm — 500 kg @ Rs 68.50/kg Total: Rs 34,250 + GST 18% Delivery: Surat by Tuesday Valid: 48 hours | Terms: Advance Reply SEND · EDIT · SKIP
Rajesh
SEND
Pakka Quote
Proposal sent. Follow-up scheduled: Day 2, Day 5, Day 10.

Conversation 3 — Follow-up (automated)

Business-initiated outbound messages on behalf of the SMB owner. Sent via email for MVP (avoids WhatsApp template approval complexity). Three touches: warm nudge, soft check-in, close the loop.

Follow-up email sequence
Day 2 — Warm nudge
Hi [Name], following up on our proposal for 500kg TMT Fe500. Happy to clarify specs or adjust terms if needed.
Day 5 — Soft check-in
Hi [Name], just checking if the proposal is still relevant. We can hold the pricing for 2 more days.
Day 10 — Close the loop
Hi [Name], should we keep this inquiry open or close it out? Happy to revisit when you're ready.

07 — Requirements

Feature requirements

F1 — Inquiry ingestion

Accept forwarded WhatsApp messages from the owner to the Pakka Quote number
Poll or webhook-receive emails from configured business email address
Support text, voice notes (transcribed via Whisper), images (via Vision), and PDF attachments
Deduplicate inquiries from the same sender within a 24-hour window

F2 — AI inquiry parser

Extract: product name, quantity, unit, specifications, delivery location, delivery deadline, urgency signals
Handle Hindi, Hinglish, and mixed-language input reliably
If extraction confidence is low, ask the owner one clarifying question before proceeding
Match parsed product against the owner's catalog to suggest relevant items
Target: 90%+ correct extraction on real inquiry corpus (validated before build)

F3 — Owner approval flow

Send structured summary to owner's WhatsApp: product, qty, specs, delivery, urgency
Accept price input as a plain number (e.g. "68.50") — no formatting required
Show draft proposal summary before sending — owner must explicitly approve
Support three commands: SEND (send as-is), EDIT (re-prompt for changes), SKIP (archive without sending)
Timeout after 4 hours without response — flag as pending in system

F4 — Proposal PDF generator

Generate professional PDF with: business name, logo, GST number, line items, total, GST breakdown, payment terms, validity period
Proposal number auto-incremented per business (e.g. SST-2026-047)
Branding sourced from onboarding — name, logo, colors
Delivered to buyer via email with PDF attachment and plain-text body
Sent from owner's configured email address (not a generic Pakka Quote domain)

F5 — Follow-up automation

Schedule follow-up emails at day 2, 5, and 10 after proposal send
Cancel sequence automatically if buyer replies to any email
Notify owner via WhatsApp when buyer replies to a follow-up
Owner can cancel a follow-up sequence by replying STOP to the WhatsApp notification
08 — Technical

Architecture

All customers share a single Pakka Quote WhatsApp number (Innovatily's verified account). The system routes by phone number to identify which business account an incoming message belongs to. Proposals go to buyers via email, not WhatsApp.

Message routing
Buyer inquiry → Owner's personal WhatsApp
Owner forwards → Pakka Quote number (Innovatily)
               ↓
Webhook identifies owner by phone number
               ↓
AI parses → Summary sent back to owner
Owner replies with price
               ↓
PDF generated → Sent to buyer via email
Celery schedules follow-up jobs

11 — Validation

Validate before building

Run the entire Pakka Quote workflow manually for 2 weeks before writing a line of code. You are the AI. Your personal WhatsApp is the product number. Canva or Word is the PDF generator.

The manual sprint — 2 weeks

Recruit 1 SMB owner from your network — offer the service free for 2 weeks
They forward real inquiries to your WhatsApp number
You manually parse, summarize, collect price, generate PDF in Canva, send via email
Collect every real inquiry — target 50 samples for the parser corpus
Document every point of friction: where does the owner get confused? What do they ask?
At the end of week 2, ask if they'd pay ₹999/month for this. That's your go/no-go signal.
What you learn: Real inquiry language and format variance, exactly which conversation steps break down, what the owner actually cares about, whether the proposal quality is good enough to impress the buyer — all before spending a single day building.

15 — Risk

Key risks

Risk
Parser fails on real Hinglish/voice inquiry data
Mitigation
Collect 50 real samples during manual sprint. Test before building parser.
Risk
Owner loses trust on first wrong price match
Mitigation
Owner manually sets all prices in v1. Zero auto-pricing until trust is established.
Risk
SMB owners expect free tools or one-time payment
Mitigation
Validate willingness to pay during manual sprint before any code. Offer ₹500/proposal first.
Risk
WhatsApp message template rejection delays follow-up feature
Mitigation
Submit templates day 1. Email-only follow-ups for MVP — no dependency on template approval.
Risk
Old Innovatily WhatsApp number has quality rating issues
Mitigation
Check phone number status in Business Manager before building. Resolve before day 1.
16 — Unknowns

Open questions

These must be answered — ideally during the manual sprint — before the full build begins.

Q1
Which industry vertical to target first — steel, textiles, chemicals, FMCG? Choose based on existing network, not market size.
Q2
What does the complete onboarding conversation look like end to end? What questions can be skipped and inferred later?
Q3
How to structure the state machine for multi-turn WhatsApp conversations? What happens if the owner sends an unrecognized reply mid-flow?
Q4
How to handle GST, freight, and payment term variations per customer? Are these stored in the catalog or entered per-proposal?
Q5
Should the system support counter-offer flows — where the owner revises a proposal after buyer pushes back? (Likely v2, but needs definition.)
Q6
What does the proposal template look like per industry? Is one flexible template enough for v1, or do steel vs textiles require different structures?
Q7
What is the data privacy and security stance? Where does inquiry and buyer data live, who owns it, and how is this communicated to SMB owners?
